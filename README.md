# ANTARES

> A Bio-Inspired Decentralized AIoT Framework for Collective Disaster Intelligence

## Vision

ANTARES is a research initiative exploring how everyday smartphones can work together as a decentralized sensing network to detect natural hazards and coordinate emergency response—without relying on centralized infrastructure.

Instead of treating each smartphone as an isolated device, ANTARES views every phone as an intelligent sensing node capable of observing its surroundings, analyzing local sensor data, and collaborating with nearby devices to reach a collective decision.

Inspired by swarm intelligence, ANTARES enables communities to build resilient, self-organizing disaster intelligence networks using devices that people already carry every day.

## The Problem

Conventional disaster warning systems often depend on expensive infrastructure, internet connectivity, or centralized processing. During large-scale disasters, these systems may become unavailable precisely when they are needed most.

Millions of smartphones already contain powerful sensors—including accelerometers, gyroscopes, microphones, barometers, and GPS receivers—but they rarely work together.

ANTARES explores a new paradigm: transforming these disconnected devices into a collaborative, distributed intelligence network.

## Core Idea

Each smartphone independently:

- Collects local sensor observations
- Uses on-device AI to estimate hazard probability
- Shares confidence scores with nearby devices through peer-to-peer communication
- Participates in decentralized consensus
- Generates community-level alerts when sufficient evidence is available

Rather than trusting a single sensor, ANTARES relies on collective intelligence emerging from many independent observations.

## Initial Research Areas

- Earthquake detection
- Landslide detection
- Flash flood monitoring
- Forest fire detection
- Decentralized consensus algorithms
- Smartphone sensor fusion
- Edge AI inference
- Peer-to-peer mesh networking

## Long-Term Vision

ANTARES aims to become a resilient disaster intelligence platform capable of operating even when traditional communication infrastructure is unavailable.

Beyond hazard detection, the framework could support emergency coordination, distributed environmental monitoring, and other applications where communities benefit from decentralized collective intelligence.
